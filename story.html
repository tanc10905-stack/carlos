LUXURY TRAVEL STORYTELLING
Turning One Journey into a Story You Can Feel
Using photos, travel diaries and long-form stories to let guests “stay once in their mind” before they ever arrive.
Through photos and copy, travel diaries and long-form stories, I break down a single journey into four emotional stages: “imagination before departure – emotions upon arrival – the cared-for details during the stay – the lingering attachment after leaving.”

Instead of only reading a brief camp introduction, the audience can “mentally stay once first, then decide whether to book”. This kind of storytelling is especially important for luxury tented camps in remote destinations, where trust, safety and emotional resonance matter as much as the hardware itself.
HOW THIS APPLIES TO A CAMP LIKE KUMBUKUMBU
  • Before departure: content answers real questions and builds a sense of calm anticipation, not anxiety.
  • On arrival: storytelling focuses on first impressions – sounds, light, landscape and the feeling of “I am really here”.
  • During the stay: details of care, staff warmth, and small surprises become the emotional highlights of the story.
  • After leaving: the guest carries away specific images and feelings that make them want to recommend or come back.
My role is to turn the camp’s daily reality into shareable, high-aesthetic narratives – so that Chinese travelers feel they already understand the atmosphere, safety and emotional value of the stay, even before they book.
奢旅内容叙事
把一次旅程，讲成用户能“住进去”的故事
通过图文、旅拍日记与长篇故事，让用户在出发之前，先在内容里“住过一次”营地。
通过图文、旅拍日记与长篇故事，把一次旅程拆解为 “出发前的想象—抵达当下的情绪—被照顾的细节—离开后的留恋” 四个阶段， 让用户不是只看一篇营地介绍，而是可以在内容里先“住过一次，再决定要不要出发”。

对于偏远目的地的奢华帐篷营地来说，这种叙事尤为重要—— 它不仅传达硬件和景观，更是在远距离的情况下，提前建立安全感、信任感与被照顾的期待。
当应用在类似 KumbuKumbu 这样的营地时
  • 出发前：用内容回答真实疑问，让用户从“不确定”变成“有点期待”。
  • 抵达时：用叙事放大第一眼看到的光线、声音、动物与空间，让“我真的到了”这件事变得有记忆点。
  • 停留中：把服务中的小细节、被照顾的瞬间、人与自然的相处感受写进故事，而不是只写“项目清单”。
  • 离开后：让用户心里留下一两幅画面、一两个片段，愿意自然而然地分享与推荐。
我的工作，是把营地每天发生的真实日常，提炼成有画面感、可分享、符合奢旅审美的内容， 让中国用户在看到内容时，就已经能感受到这里的氛围、安全感与情绪价值。
